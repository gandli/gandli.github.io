Boss proposed a project born from real pain points: an intelligent medication management tool for chronic disease patients and their families.

### Pain Points

- Patients don't understand prescriptions from doctors, medication instructions don't speak human language
- Multi-medication management is chaotic (chronic disease patients take 5-16 medications)
- Drug interaction risks are invisible
- Medication sources are diverse: hospital prescriptions, in-hospital preparations, pharmacy purchases, traditional Chinese medicine

### Competitive Research

The market isn't emptyâ€”Alipay, Baidu, Huawei have all entered:
- Alipay AI medicine box recognition
- AI digital human pharmacists (multiple tertiary hospital mini programs)
- Baidu AI medication instructions
- Huawei Xiaoyi translation assistant

But they're all doing "look it up," nobody is doing "manage it."

### Differentiation

1. **Multi-medication full-cycle management** (not single medication lookup)
2. **Full coverage of Chinese and Western medicine** (TCM decoctions/patent medicines/in-hospital preparations)
3. **Family collaboration** (children remotely supervising parents' medication)
4. **Safety detection** (multi-drug interactions + Chinese-Western medicine conflicts + liver/kidney burden)

Tech stack: WeChat mini program + Supabase + large model AI interpretation.

### Naming

Ran a round of trademark checks, "MedMate" and "YaoHua" are available, others have similar products.

Chrome Store rejected AI Proofreader Duck, reason: features described (summary, polishing, error correction, translation, expansion) don't work.

Most likely the reviewer's browser doesn't have Chrome Built-in AI (Gemini Nano) enabled, nor API Key configured, so all features are unavailable.

Solution direction: add a free fallback API to ensure out-of-box usability + modify description to indicate prerequisites.
